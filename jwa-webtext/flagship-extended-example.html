The question

Start with a ranking

“Counting only the citations in the index, which work does the field cite most?” The Most Cited page answers directly: Natasha Jones’s “The Technical Communicator as Advocate” (2016) leads at 159 internal citations, then Jones, Moore & Walton (2016) at 155, then David Russell’s “Rethinking Genre in School and Society” (1997) at 146.

But a ranking hides a puzzle. Russell’s article had almost three decades to accrete citations; the two works above it had only eight years, and Jones authored both. Is that two unusually fast articles, or a larger shift reorganizing the field?

Do they hold together?

Ask what’s cited alongside what

Co-citation records which works the field cites simultaneously. The most co-cited author pair in the entire index is Jones & Walton, at 128 joint citations; beneath them, Agboka with Jones, Agboka with Walton, Moore with Jones — all scholars working the same area.

So the two fast risers aren’t isolated. They travel with a recognizable group. But co-citation shows association, not structure — so the question has to move again.

Is it a real community?

Partition the whole network

Community detection splits the citation network into groups whose members cite one another more densely than they cite the rest. Read all-time, the field’s center of gravity is still the older genre-and-social-construction cluster that Russell, Bruffee, and Schryer anchor; the social-justice technical-communication community sits second.

Now restrict the partition to 2010–present. Genre-and-social-construction drops out of the top tier entirely — while social-justice TC holds its place near the top. The recent field is organized differently from the accumulated one.

At what scale, and when?

Watch the network consolidate

Temporal network evolution shows the shift as change over time, not in total. Across the last three five-year windows the largest connected component grows from 29% of nodes to 43% and then 60%, and internal citations per window roughly double. More and more indexed articles belong to one interlinked web instead of sitting on disconnected islands.

This happens in precisely the years the Jones, Haas, and Agboka articles enter the graph and receive citational uptake. Toggle modularity: it stays high even as the component fills — the field knits together without dissolving its neighborhoods.

The claim, assembled

Social-justice technical communication has become the structural center of the field’s citation network — and reached that position faster than the older canonical moments did.

No single tool makes that claim. Most Cited surfaced two fast risers; co-citation showed they travel as a group; all-time community detection placed the group second; and the period-restricted partition plus the temporal view showed it moving to the center as the recent network consolidates around it. The consolidation of the field into one connected core and the rise of this particular cluster are the same event at two scales.

If you already knew this as someone in the field, that’s the point: Pinakes lets us quantify the knowing and ground anecdotal disciplinary sense in data anyone can re-run.
