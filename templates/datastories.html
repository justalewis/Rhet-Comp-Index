Datastories

Chapter-organized analyses

Twenty-six analytical tools, organized by the chapter of Datastories they produced figures for. Each tool is the live interactive form of a CLI script that ran during the book's research phase. Click a chapter to expand its tools.

The Braided Path
A Sankey of citation flows between TPC and rhetoric/composition by decade.
Branching Traditions
The 49 indexed journals classified by tradition (TPC, RC, adjacent), showing how the corpus is composed.
Origins and Frontiers
Sources (foundational works no one cites further) and sinks (current frontier articles); how the field's chronological edges look.
Shifting Currents
Per-decade Hummon-Doreian main paths showing how the field's central knowledge thread moves over time.
The Speed of Influence
How long it takes citations to land — comparing TPC→TPC, TPC→RC, RC→TPC, RC→RC delay distributions.
Border Crossers
Articles with high betweenness centrality at the TPC/RC divide — the structural bridges between sub-fields.
The Two-Way Street
Reciprocity rates between TPC and RC: how often citation goes both ways, and which articles are most-reciprocated.
The Shape of Influence
Lorenz curve, Gini coefficient, and power-law fit for the citation distribution.
The Long Tail
Per-article concentration metrics — how broadly versus narrowly each canonical article is cited.
The Fair Ranking
Citations per year of life: who rises when you control for the age advantage of older articles?
Shifting Canons
Top-cited articles per generation of citing scholars: enduring canon, rising stars, fading classics.
The Reach of a Citation
Per-article citation accumulation curves — steady classics, late bloomers, one-wave articles.
Inside and Outside
Internal (corpus) versus global (OpenAlex) ranking: TPC-specific canon vs. articles imported but not metabolized.
Communities Over Time
Per-decade Louvain communities and an alluvial flow showing how groups split, merge, and persist.
Walls and Bridges
Per-community insularity ratio: which communities cite themselves, which look outward.
The First Spark
Key-route main-path analysis: not one main thread but several parallel high-traffic routes through the citation DAG.
Shared Foundations
Bibliographic coupling network: articles that cite the same prior works.
Two Maps of the Field
Coupling clusters versus citation communities — where the two views agree and where they diverge.
The Books Everyone Reads
Articles that show up across the most chapters' figures — a derived "master works" list for the book project.
Uneven Debts
Asymmetric coupling: pairs where one article depends on the other's reading much more than the reverse.
Solo to Squad
Team-size trends over time: when single-authorship lost majority status, when 5+ author articles arrived.
Academic Lineages
Probable mentor → mentee relationships inferred from publication-year gaps in co-authorship.
Lasting Partnerships
Co-authoring pairs classified by persistence: one-shot, short-term, and long-running collaborations.
Prince Network
For each sleeping beauty, which articles ("princes") woke it up, and what kind of awakening it was.
The Disciplinary Calendar
Curated disciplinary events laid against sleeping-beauty awakening spikes.
The Unread Canon
Articles cited heavily in the wider scholarly world but rarely cited inside the field's own corpus.
